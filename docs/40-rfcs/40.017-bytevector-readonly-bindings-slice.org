#+TITLE: Bytevector Read-only Bindings Slice
#+DATE: 2026-07-21
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil

* Scope

This slice adds a read-only runtime-backed Scheme bytevector projection to the
generic =gerbil-scheme-rust= bindings library.

It is intentionally smaller than the complete byte/u8vector utility family.
The following remain open until ownership, allocation, mutation, endian, and
signedness policies are specified:

- =u8vector-init!=
- =u8vector-every=
- =u8vector-reverse= and =u8vector-reverse!=
- =u8vector->bytestring= and =bytestring->u8vector=
- =u8vector->uint= and =uint->u8vector=
- =u8vector->sint= and =sint->u8vector=
- =bytevector->bytestring= and =bytestring->bytevector=
- =bytevector->uint= and =uint->bytevector=

* Accepted surface

- Scheme source exports a =#u8(...)= fixture through
  =gerbil-rs-fixture-bytevector-raw=.
- Scheme source exports bytevector classification through
  =gerbil-rs-scheme-object-bytevector?-raw=.
- Scheme source exports read-only byte accessors:
  - =gerbil-rs-scheme-object-bytevector-length-raw=
  - =gerbil-rs-scheme-object-bytevector-u8-ref-raw=
- =gerbil-scheme-sys= owns the bytevector ABI in a focused module instead of
  growing =abi.rs=.
- =gerbil-scheme= exposes =SchemeBytevector=,
  =GerbilValue::is_bytevector=, =GerbilValue::as_bytevector=,
  and =GerbilRuntime::fixture_bytevector_value=.
- =SchemeBytevector= exposes =len=, =is_empty=, =u8_at=, and =to_vec=.

* Boundary decisions

The first accepted bytevector surface is read-only.

Mutation and allocation are deferred because they need separate contracts for:

- whether Rust or Gerbil owns allocated byte storage;
- whether a returned bytevector is rooted or only borrowed;
- whether mutation is in-place against a Gerbil-owned object;
- how errors are represented for index, endian, signedness, and overflow;
- whether conversions use strings, byte strings, or raw byte buffers.

* Runtime fixture

The accepted fixture bytes are:

#+begin_src scheme
#u8(255 127 11 1 0)
#+end_src

This fixture intentionally covers high byte, ASCII byte, small byte, one, and
zero.
